Why Verification Matters in Card Payments

Verification ensures that the person using a card is the legitimate cardholder. Without proper verification layers, fraud risk increases dramatically — especially online.

  • Identity verification at onboarding (KYC)
  • Ongoing risk scoring during transactions
  • Authentication checks like biometrics or PIN
  • Device and behavioral analysis

Card systems combine multiple verification layers to keep payments smooth while still blocking suspicious behavior.

KYC & Identity Proofing

KYC (“Know Your Customer”) checks confirm your identity when opening a card account. Common steps include:

  • Document verification (passport, ID, driver’s license)
  • Biometric checks (selfie or liveness detection)
  • Database checks (sanctions, PEP lists)
  • Address verification

These checks help prevent identity theft and ensure the account is tied to a verified individual.

Real-Time Transaction Verification

During a payment, verification steps can include:

  • Risk engine scoring
  • Device fingerprinting
  • Location matching
  • Velocity checks (repeat attempts, unusually high spending)
  • Behavioral checks (typing patterns, app behavior)

Most of these happen invisibly — only high-risk transactions trigger extra steps.

Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

SCA typically uses two of three factors:

  • Something you know (PIN/password)
  • Something you have (device, secure element)
  • Something you are (biometrics)

Good SCA design minimizes friction while preserving strong security.
