What does “verify” mean in card programs?

Verification is any extra step the issuer takes to be sure about a person, a device or a transaction. Sometimes at onboarding, sometimes when something looks risky.

  • • Verifying your identity (KYC & digital ID)
  • • Verifying a new device or browser
  • • Verifying a high-risk transaction
  • • Verifying changes to limits, address or contact details
  • • Verifying card details have not been stolen

Types of verification

Identity verification

ID documents, selfies, national eID or bank ID to confirm who you are.

Device verification

SMS, email link, push approval or app-based checks when you log in from a new device.

Payment verification

3-D Secure and Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) for higher-risk transactions.

Change verification

Extra steps when changing phone number, email, address or limits.

When do issuers ask for extra verification?

Scenario Typical verification
New device login SMS code, push approval, email link or biometric via app.
Unusual purchase 3-D Secure challenge or in-app confirmation.
Address / contact change Re-authentication plus one-time code.
Limit increase Full re-check of ID, income and risk profile.
Suspected fraud Contact from issuer, card lock and full identity re-check.

Why verification matters

  • • Stops attackers from taking over your account
  • • Reduces misuse of stolen card details
  • • Helps issuers trust new devices and channels
  • • Required by regulators for certain risk levels
  • • Enables smoother experiences once trust is built

Good vs. bad verification UX

  • • Good: clear explanation of why verification is needed
  • • Good: multiple secure options (app, push, biometrics)
  • • Good: minimal friction for low-risk actions
  • • Bad: generic error messages and repeated SMS loops
  • • Bad: no clear way to contact support if you are blocked
